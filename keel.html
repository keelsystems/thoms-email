Who I work with

Keel Systems is for people who are stuck between “do nothing” and “hire a full-time IT department”. If any of these sound familiar, you’re in the right place:

  • A small company where IT is “whoever knows the Wi-Fi password”.
  • A home user with a growing lab who wants it to stop feeling fragile.
  • A team that outgrew a single router and shared inbox but doesn’t know what to fix first.

What I can help you with

Small business networks and Wi-Fi

  • Clean, labeled network layout (modem, router, switches, APs, VLANs if needed).
  • Reliable Wi-Fi coverage with clear SSID strategy and guest access where it makes sense.
  • Basic firewall rules and remote access that don’t require a CCNA to maintain.

Microsoft 365 and SharePoint

  • Move shared drives into SharePoint with a folder structure that fits how you work.
  • Permissions that make sense, with fewer “everyone has access to everything” situations.
  • Simple flows using Power Automate for approvals, notifications, or recurring tasks.

Home labs and self-hosted services

  • Proxmox, containers, and a reasonable number of VMs instead of an unmanageable zoo.
  • Services like Nextcloud and Jellyfin behind sane DNS, certificates, and authentication.
  • Tailscale or similar for secure, low-friction remote access.

Lightweight automation and reporting

  • PowerShell and Python scripts to clean up repetitive tasks and reporting.
  • Simple dashboards or exports that give you a clearer picture without needing a BI team.

Example engagements

Local business with scattered files

Email on one system, files on a mix of USB drives, Google accounts, and a dusty office PC. We map out what you actually use, get Microsoft 365 or another platform set up properly, and migrate everything with a clear folder structure and permissions.

Resort or campground with shaky Wi-Fi

Point-to-point links, consumer routers, and a lot of guesswork. We document what’s there, design a simpler topology, and improve coverage and stability so guests and staff can just get online.

Home power user with a growing lab

A server or two, some containers, and a feeling that one mistake could knock everything over. We tidy your layout, add backups, and put services behind a sensible networking and DNS setup.

How a typical project runs

Every environment is different, but the basic flow stays the same so you always know what’s next.

1

Short intake

You tell me what hurts today, what gear you already have, and what “fixed” would feel like.

2

Map and plan

I map your current setup, propose a practical target state, and we confirm scope and priorities.

3

Build and clean up

We implement changes in a controlled way, with backups and rollback paths where it matters.

4

Docs and hand-off

You get diagrams, notes, and simple runbooks so you’re not dependent on me forever.
